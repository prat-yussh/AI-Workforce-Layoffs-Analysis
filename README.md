# AI and Workforce Layoffs Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes workforce layoffs across industries between 2024 and 2026. The analysis focuses on layoffs, AI adoption, AI replacement risk, employee sentiment, job security, hiring trends, and business performance.

The objective is to understand workforce transformation trends and identify relationships between layoffs and organizational factors using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

## Dataset Overview

* Records: 12,000
* Features: 23
* Period: 2024–2026

Key variables include:

* Layoffs Count
* Layoff Percentage
* AI Adoption Level
* AI Replacement Risk
* Employee Sentiment
* Job Security Score
* Revenue Growth
* Open Roles
* Remote Jobs Percentage
* Salary Budget Change

## Tools Used

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

* Social Media and AI industries experienced the highest workforce reductions.
* Layoffs remained consistently high between 2024 and 2026.
* Job security showed the strongest negative relationship with layoffs.
* Employee sentiment decreased as layoffs increased.
* AI replacement risk was positively associated with layoffs.
* AI adoption itself showed almost no direct relationship with layoffs.

## Visualizations

* Bar Charts
* Scatter Plots
* Line Charts
* Histograms
* Box Plots
* Correlation Heatmaps

## Conclusion

The analysis suggests that workforce reductions are influenced by multiple business and organizational factors. While AI replacement risk is moderately related to layoffs, AI adoption alone does not directly lead to workforce reductions. Employee sentiment and job security remain important indicators of workforce stability.
