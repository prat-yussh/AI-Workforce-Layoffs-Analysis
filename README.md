# AI and Workforce Layoffs Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes workforce layoffs across industries between 2024 and 2026. The analysis focuses on layoffs, AI adoption, AI replacement risk, employee sentiment, job security, hiring trends, and company performance.

The objective is to understand workforce transformation trends and identify relationships between layoffs and organizational factors using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

---

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains **12,000 records** and **23 features** related to layoffs, hiring activity, AI adoption, automation impact, employee sentiment, job security, and company performance across multiple industries.

### Key Variables

* Layoffs Count
* Layoff Percentage
* AI Adoption Level
* AI Replacement Risk
* AI Automation Impact
* Employee Sentiment

## Key Findings

1. Social Media and AI industries experienced the highest workforce reductions.
2. Layoffs remained relatively stable between 2024 and 2026.
3. Layoff distributions were highly right-skewed, with a few companies experiencing extremely large workforce reductions.
4. AI, Cloud, and Gaming industries showed high AI adoption while maintaining relatively strong employee sentiment.
5. Job security score showed the strongest negative relationship with layoffs (-0.47).
6. Employee sentiment decreased as layoffs increased (-0.30).
7. AI replacement risk showed a moderate positive relationship with layoffs (0.30).
8. AI adoption level showed almost no direct relationship with layoffs (0.003).
9. Social Media displayed the widest variation in layoff activity.
10. Open roles were negatively correlated with layoffs (-0.28).

## Conclusion

The analysis suggests that workforce reductions are influenced by multiple business and organizational factors. While AI replacement risk is moderately associated with layoffs, AI adoption itself does not directly lead to workforce reductions.

Employee sentiment and job security remain important indicators of workforce stability, highlighting the human impact of workforce transformation during periods of technological change.
